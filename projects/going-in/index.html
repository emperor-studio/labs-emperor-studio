Salon exhibition concept

Going In

Curated by Dylan Hale Lewis Popup gallery + salon Limited edition printed book

Going In is conceived as a popup gallery show and intimate salon, curated by Dylan Hale Lewis as a temporary cultural world rather than a conventional exhibition. The event is built around the feeling of crossing a threshold, into image, atmosphere, conversation, and the deeper symbolic or emotional terrain the work opens up.

Concept

Part exhibition, part gathering, part ritual, Going In would bring together selected works, spatial atmosphere, lighting, objects, and a carefully cultivated guest experience into a singular evening or short-run series of evenings.

Tone

The event should feel elegant, cinematic, slightly uncanny, and culturally alive, with the sense that being there matters. Guests are not simply attending a show, they are entering an environment shaped with intention.

Salon dimension

The salon dimension is essential: conversation, intimacy, and social texture are part of the work’s meaning. In this form, Going In becomes more than a popup exhibition. It becomes a temporary social and aesthetic world, authored by Dylan, entered by invitation, and remembered through both presence and artifact.

Exhibition

Selected works presented not as a neutral hang, but as a narrative environment with pacing, threshold, and emotional charge.

Gathering

An invite-based social layer with intimate conversation, carefully tuned atmosphere, and the feeling of entry into a private cultural world.

Afterlife

A printed object that carries the evening forward, extending the show beyond attendance and into memory, collection, and private ownership.

Limited edition printed book

A key part of the concept is a limited-edition printed book created specifically for the show. Rather than a generic catalog, it would function as a keepsake artifact, something between an artist book, exhibition document, and collectible object. The book could feature selected images, fragments of text, curatorial notes, or other material shaped specifically for the experience of Going In.

Ideally, it would be produced in a numbered limited edition and made available only to attendees, collectors, or a small invited circle, giving the salon a material afterlife and reinforcing the sense of rarity and intimacy.

The event, the gathering, and the printed book all work together as one cultural gesture.
