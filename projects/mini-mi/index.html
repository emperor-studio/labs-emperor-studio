Mini Mi · private AI installation

An assistant with a home.

Mini Mi is a private AI setup for people who have too much context living in too many places: texts, calendars, notes, files, half-finished ideas, and people waiting for an answer. We install a dedicated Mac mini, give the assistant a clear job, connect the channels that matter, and keep tuning it until it starts to feel useful in real life.

Not another chatbot subscription. Not a glossy tech pitch. A small, cared-for system that lives with the work.

The simple version

A managed personal AI box: memory, messages, reminders, projects, specialists, and someone responsible for keeping it sane.

The problem

Most AI helps for ten minutes, then forgets the room.

You explain the situation. It gives a decent answer. Then the next time, you explain it again. The person, the project, the weird constraint, the conversation from last week, the file you meant to send — all of it has to be reassembled by hand.

Mini Mi is built around a different idea: the assistant should have continuity. It should know where things live, remember decisions, notice when something needs follow-up, and be humble enough to ask before acting when the stakes are real.

The Manny Mo test

If it only impresses AI people, it does not count.

The Manny Mo conversation sharpened the standard: this has to make sense to a busy human who does not want to manage a science project. The product is not “look at all these models.” The product is fewer dropped balls.

Can it remember who needs a reply? Can it find the note from the last conversation? Can it prep the next step without pretending it has authority it does not have? Can it explain, plainly, what broke when something breaks?

Mini Mi should feel less like software you operate and more like a quiet back office that knows your name, your projects, and your tolerance for nonsense.

Email and message triage, with drafts or plans prepared before anything is sent
Project memory: what we decided, where the file is, who owns the next step
Research, writing, summaries, and rough thinking that turns into usable material
Specialist assistants for focused areas: business ideas, household ops, design, language learning, estate work
A local backup brain that can still answer basic questions when cloud AI services hiccup

How it behaves

Useful, careful, and allowed to say “I need approval.”

The assistant can prepare, summarize, compare, remind, organize, draft, and investigate. It should not quietly send emails, change important settings, or act like it owns your life. The system is designed around a simple trust rule: be proactive where it is safe, ask when it matters.

Private first

Your context stays in a system configured for you, with clear boundaries about what should and should not leave the machine.

Multi-model, quietly

Strong models for hard work, cheaper models for light checks, and local models for basic resilience. The client should not have to care unless something changes.

Human-readable

Important memory and operating notes should be inspectable. If the assistant “knows” something, there should be a place to find it.

Who it is for

Households and estates

Families, homes, and properties where vendors, schedules, travel, documents, and recurring decisions need continuity.

Small operators

Founders, consultants, producers, artists, and independent teams who need a practical second brain before they need enterprise software.

The offer

Installation, configuration, and stewardship.

Mini Mi is sold less like an app and more like a white-glove setup: hardware, installation, identity, memory structure, channels, workflows, backups, and ongoing care. The first version does not need to promise magic. It needs to become genuinely handy.

The best outcome is quiet: fewer missed follow-ups, fewer repeated explanations, fewer “where did we put that?” moments, and a person who feels a little less alone carrying the admin layer of their life.

Mini Mi is for the person who does not want to become an AI hobbyist. They want someone to put the machine in the corner, teach it the shape of their world, and keep it useful.
