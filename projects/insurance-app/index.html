Labs concept page

Insurance, finally visible.

A concept for an app that turns scattered policies, brokers, premiums, riders, deductibles, and overlapping coverage into one clear control panel, so people can understand what they have, spot waste, and model smarter decisions.

Core idea
Coverage mapping, overlap analysis, and scenario modeling for messy insurance stacks.

The problem

Most insurance stacks are fragmented and opaque

People often have multiple policies with multiple providers and brokers, spread across home, auto, umbrella, business, liability, equipment, property, and specialty coverage. Premiums keep getting paid, but the actual coverage picture stays blurry.

What people cannot easily answer

  • What am I actually covered for?
  • Where am I double-covered?
  • Where am I exposed?
  • What is costing too much?
  • What could be consolidated?
  • What changes would affect premium and exposure?

What the app does

1. Ingest

Collect and read policy documents

  • Upload declarations pages, full policy PDFs, endorsements, schedules, and broker notes
  • Capture provider, policy number, term, premium, deductible, limits, riders, and exclusions
  • Turn ugly documents into structured data

2. Normalize

Create a unified coverage map

  • Map assets, entities, locations, and risks across policies
  • Group similar coverage types into one readable system
  • Show which assets are covered where, and by whom

3. Reveal

Make the stack visible at a glance

  • Total annual premium across all policies
  • Provider and broker map
  • Coverage by asset, category, and location
  • Overlap flags, gap flags, and upcoming renewals

4. Model

Test scenarios before calling the broker

  • Move assets between policies in a sandbox
  • Raise or lower deductibles with simple sliders
  • Compare what premium and exposure might look like under different structures
  • Identify which questions to take back to the broker or advisor

What the experience could feel like

Coverage dashboard

  • Annual premium rollup
  • Active policies and renewal dates
  • Coverage categories and limits
  • Assets tied to specific policies
  • Notes, uncertainties, and broker questions

Overlap and gap analysis

  • Potential duplicate coverage
  • Coverage that appears thin or absent
  • Policies that may exist mainly out of inertia
  • Places where a rider may be unnecessary

Scenario sandbox

The app does not need to pretend it replaces an insurance professional. Its value is clarity. A smart sandbox could help users ask better questions, see likely tradeoffs, and understand whether a change might simplify the stack, reduce waste, or shift risk in a way worth discussing.

Why this is useful

For households

People with multiple homes, cars, assets, or specialty coverage often lose track of the total picture. This creates a real sense of uncertainty and often unnecessary spend.

For business owners

Businesses accumulate policies over time, often through separate events and separate advisors. What starts as prudent coverage can become a confusing patchwork.

For brokers and advisors

This could also become a better client-facing review surface. Instead of reading opaque PDFs together, broker and client could look at one map of reality and discuss changes from a shared picture.

Important posture

This is not fake magic insurance autopilot

The smartest version of this product is not “AI replaces your broker.” It is a decision-support layer that makes policy stacks understandable, exposes inefficiencies, surfaces ambiguity, and helps people ask the right questions before making real changes.

Better promise

  • Coverage clarity
  • Portfolio visibility
  • Overlap detection
  • Scenario comparison
  • Smarter broker conversations

Who it is for

  • High-complexity households
  • People with multiple policies across multiple providers
  • Small businesses with layered insurance needs
  • Studios, property owners, and operators with mixed asset classes
  • Brokers who want a clearer client review interface

Potential v1

Start with clarity, not optimization theater

  • Document upload
  • Structured policy summary cards
  • Premium rollup
  • Coverage-by-asset map
  • Overlap and gap flags
  • Renewal calendar
  • Broker questions and notes

Then add modeling

  • Deductible and limit sliders
  • Asset-move sandbox
  • Policy comparison views
  • Confidence labels where interpretation is uncertain
  • Decision memos users can bring into broker conversations
