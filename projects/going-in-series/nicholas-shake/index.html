Going In: The Series

Nicholas Shake

A living publication entry centered on process, preparation, material transformation, and the strange interval between image, language, and object.

Series frame

Going In: The Series is conceived as a living publication in which Dylan enters into intimate exchange with selected artists, then publishes that encounter across print, photography, moving image, and other forms of documentation. Each edition is less an interview than an immersion into an artist’s working logic.

This edition

With Nicholas Shake, the emphasis should fall heavily on preparation and process, not only the finished works, but the buildup, layering, repetition, decision-making, and material states that make the paintings possible.

Curatorial emphasis

Nicholas Shake’s work carries a charged instability between text and image, language and atmosphere, surface and residue. What matters here is not merely the final painting as object, but the sequence of acts that bring it into being, staining, masking, layering, obscuring, revealing, and pushing language through material until it becomes both legible and haunted.

Preparation

The publication should document what comes before the work resolves: source material, visual fragments, language choices, surfaces, references, color testing, and the physical setup required to enter a piece.

Process

The strongest material may be transitional rather than conclusive, cropped details, partial states, studio views, and the layered intervals where the work is still deciding what it is.

Publication form

This edition could live as a printed zine or folio, supported by still photography and short-form video that privileges gesture, handling, sequence, and the emotional weather of the studio.

What to document

The publication should pay close attention to the artist in contact with his materials: preparing grounds, building surfaces, selecting words, moving between control and accident, and letting images emerge through sedimented action. The goal is not simply to explain the work but to let viewers feel the intelligence of its making.

Print

A limited-edition printed object could combine finished works, process stills, fragments of conversation, notes, and details that reveal how the paintings are built rather than only how they look once complete.

Photo + video

Photography and video should function less as promotional documentation and more as evidence of encounter, attention, labor, hesitation, repetition, and emergence.

This edition of Going In: The Series should feel like entry into Nicholas Shake’s process, not just his finished work.
