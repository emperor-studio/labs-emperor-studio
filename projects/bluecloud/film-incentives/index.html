Above the Line vs. Below the Line

ATL (Above the Line) covers creative talent - directors, writers, producers, principal cast, stunt performers, music directors/composers/supervisors.
BTL (Below the Line) covers production crew, equipment, locations, facilities, post-production labor, and services.

CA key insight: California Program 4.0 deliberately excludes ATL wages (directors, writers, producers, principal cast) from qualified expenditures. The credit is structured as a below-the-line incentive - rewarding productions that employ California crew, use California facilities, and spend money with California vendors. This means a $50M movie with $10M in star salaries gets the credit on the other $40M in BTL spend, not the full budget.

California - Qualified vs. Non-Qualified Expenditures

✓ Qualified Expenditures (earn the 35% credit)

  • BTL crew wages & fringe benefits
  • Background performers, stand-ins
  • Off-camera stunt personnel
  • Equipment rentals (in CA)
  • Facility & location rentals (in CA)
  • Set construction materials & labor
  • Wardrobe, props, set dressing
  • Food & lodging (production period)
  • Lab processing
  • Safety costs
  • Post-production labor & services (in CA)
  • Visual effects (in CA) - 70% wages / 30% non-wages
  • Insurance (prorated for in-state work)
  • Contingency (up to 10% of QEB)
  • Completion bond (up to 2% of QEB)

✗ Non-Qualified Expenditures (no credit)

  • ATL wages: producers, writers, directors, actors, stunt performers, music directors/composers/supervisors
  • Development costs & rights acquisition
  • Financing & overhead costs
  • Marketing, publicity & distribution
  • Digital cinema release prints
  • Residual & clip use compensation
  • Ancillary products (toys, games, trailers)
  • Services performed outside California
  • CPA/AUP audit fees
  • Federal payroll taxes (FICA, FUTA, MEDICARE)
  • CA solvency taxes
  • Expenditures before CAL issuance
  • E&O insurance
  • Career pathways program contributions
